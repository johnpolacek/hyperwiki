## Stage Goal

Make learnings contextual. A correction made while working a specific plan or lifecycle phase is most useful when it resurfaces in that context. The plumbing already exists: `SessionRecord` carries `scope`/`planPath`, and `src/lib/lifecycle.ts` derives the active phase. This stage joins learnings to that context and surfaces them where the work happens — the differentiator over a context-free CLI plugin.

<Dependencies>

- Stage 02: a working capture → approve → write loop with `LearningRecord`.
- Existing context sources: `SessionRecord` scope/planPath fields; `activeLifecyclePhase()` in `src/lib/lifecycle.ts`.

</Dependencies>

### Unit 01 — Scope learnings to plan / phase at capture

<Scope>

- Extend `LearningRecord` and the Stage 02 capture step; no transcript-layer changes.

</Scope>

<ImplementationNotes>

- At capture, read the source session's `scope`/`planPath` and the active lifecycle phase, and stamp them onto the `LearningRecord`.
- Sessions with no plan scope record a `global` learning — still valid, just unscoped.

</ImplementationNotes>

<Verification title="Verification">
  - Manual: a correction made in a plan-scoped terminal yields a learning tagged with that plan path; an unscoped session yields a `global` learning.
</Verification>

### Unit 02 — Surface scoped learnings in the UI

<Scope>

- Read-only surfacing in plan pages and the lifecycle dashboard; no writes.

</Scope>

<ImplementationNotes>

- Show a plan's approved learnings on its plan page, and phase-scoped learnings in `src/components/views/LifecycleDashboardView.tsx`, consistent with the orchestrator's "report and recommend" posture (`lifecycle-contract.md`).
- Learnings are presented as context/recommendations — never auto-applied to files from these surfaces.

</ImplementationNotes>
